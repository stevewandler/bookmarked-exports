The Challenge

Compliance Created a Data Problem

SB 13 and SB 412 require districts to review library materials — but the infrastructure to do this systematically never existed. Librarians are left with spreadsheets and guesswork.

Board-Level Liability

SB 13 didn't just create more work for librarians — it made book compliance a board-level liability. A parent challenge without a documented, defensible process becomes a crisis.

No Visibility, No Context

Districts can't see what external sources say about their books, can't benchmark against peer districts, and can't systematically assess risk across their entire collection.

The Solution

Book Intelligence — The Engine Underneath Everything

Book Intelligence aggregates everything publicly known about a book from external sources — metadata databases, public reviews, news coverage, ban and challenge lists, organization flags — and organizes it for decision-making. Every screen, every workflow, and every report in OnShelf is powered by Book Intelligence.

When your district joins Bookmarked, every title in your collection gets a comprehensive Intelligence Card: identity and metadata, content ratings, grade level suitability, external flags with source attribution, and an AI-enriched content summary — all from publicly available information about the book.
