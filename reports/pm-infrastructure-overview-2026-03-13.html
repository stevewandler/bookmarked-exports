PM Infrastructure Overview

Bookmarked Operating System · Built March 12-13, 2026 · Deployed to Sarah El Massry

TL;DR

In one session, we built a complete product management infrastructure for a company that had none. Sarah El Massry (Product Owner) has been operating for a year with no scorecard, no structured 1:1s, no ticket quality standards, and no feedback loops. We built all of it — measurables, tracking systems, operational guides, Confluence pages, automated reports, and QA-validated integrations — then deployed it to her with a single email.

The system is agent-augmented: AI agents do the data pulling, report generation, quality enforcement, and pattern detection. Humans do the judgment, relationships, and accountability conversations. Every piece connects — the scorecard feeds the 1:1, the 1:1 generates tasks, tasks feed the sprint, the sprint feeds the scorecard.

Key Design Decisions

DecisionWhat We ChoseWhy
UAT trackingJira labels, not workflow changesNo Jira admin access needed. Labels are fast, visible, measurable. Raju doesn't need to change anything.
Scorecard locationConfluence page, not spreadsheetLives where Sarah already works. Cross-linked to all related pages. Version history built in.
Sarah's guidesTwo docs: walkthrough (how) + PM guide (why)Separates reference from philosophy. She'll use the walkthrough daily; the guide is for context.
Agent autonomy/jira and /jira-standards are Level 1 (executor)Ticket creation and validation should be fast and consistent. No room for "judgment calls" on quality standards.
Sprint cadenceKeep 1-2 day sprints (not traditional 2-week)Matches Bookmarked's fast iteration speed. Scorecard adapts with per-sprint tracking.
Patrick's accountFound existing Atlassian ID (712020:7231c77e)No new account needed. UAT ticket assignment now possible.
Known gapsDocumented openly in walkthroughSarah should see what's not ready yet. Prevents surprise and builds trust.
