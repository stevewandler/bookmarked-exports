The Bookmarked Council — Operations Playbook

How the system works and how to operate it day-to-day

Internal Document — Not for Distribution
Last updated: March 23, 2026

1 System Architecture Overview

The Bookmarked Council operates across six connected systems. Partners interact through the external portal, while the team manages everything through Claude Code, which reads from and writes to the underlying data layer.

Solid lines = automated data flow  •  Dashed lines = Claude Code reads/writes  •  Orange = partner flow  •  Teal = team flow

3 How to Operate with Claude Code

Each task below shows the plain-English prompt to give Claude, what Claude does behind the scenes, and where to verify the result. Click any task to expand it.

A

Update Partner Status

▶
What you say to Claude
"Update Saddleback's status to Confirmed in the partner roster"
What Claude does
  • Opens the Confluence Partner Roster page
  • Finds the Saddleback row and updates the status column to "Confirmed"
  • Adds a timestamp note for the status change
Where to verify
Check the Confluence Partner Roster page, or open the Ops Dashboard → Partner Tracker section to see the updated status.
B

Log a Meeting or Call

▶
What you say to Claude
"I just had a call with Jade from Saddleback. Here's what we discussed: [paste notes or transcript]"
What Claude does
  • Creates a structured entry in the Meeting Log page on Confluence
  • Extracts action items from the discussion
  • Updates Partner Roster with any status changes or new contact info
  • Flags anything that needs Steve's attention
Where to verify
Check the Meeting Log page on Confluence. Action items will appear in the Feedback Tracker if they're product-related.
C

Process Incoming Feedback

▶
What you say to Claude
"A partner submitted feedback through the portal — check the Google Sheet and route it"
What Claude does
  • Reads the Google Sheet to find new, unprocessed submissions
  • Creates a structured entry in the Confluence Feedback Tracker
  • If the feedback is actionable, creates a Jira ticket linked to the Council epic
  • Tags the feedback by type: product, content, process, or general
  • Marks the row as processed in the Sheet
Where to verify
Check the Feedback Tracker on Confluence. If a Jira ticket was created, it will show in the Bookmarked project board.
D

Send Outreach Emails

▶
What you say to Claude
"Send the warm intro email to [District Name], contact is [Name] at [email]"
What Claude does
  • Pulls the appropriate email template from the District Outreach Playbook on Confluence
  • Personalizes it with the district name and contact
  • Drafts the email and shows it to Steve for approval
  • After Steve approves, sends via gws CLI
  • Updates the Partner Roster with outreach status and date
Where to verify
Check Gmail sent folder. Partner Roster on Confluence will show the outreach date and status.

Important: All external emails require Steve's approval before sending.

E

Update the Ops Dashboard

▶
What you say to Claude
"Update the recruitment table — Silverleaf ISD confirmed, contact is Jane Archer"
What Claude does
  • Edits the Ops Dashboard HTML file locally
  • Updates the relevant table with the new information
  • Copies the file to /tmp/bookmarked-exports/exports/
  • Pushes to GitHub Pages so the live dashboard reflects the change
Where to verify
Open the Ops Dashboard URL in your browser. Changes should be live within a minute of the push.
F

Prepare for a Session

▶
What you say to Claude
"Prep me for the Week 1 publisher co-design session"
What Claude does
  • Pulls the current Partner Roster — who's confirmed, their roles, their context
  • Reads recent feedback from the Feedback Tracker
  • Reviews the Meeting Log for any outstanding action items
  • Generates a branded prep brief (HTML) with key talking points, attendee info, and agenda items
  • Opens the brief on your desktop
Where to verify
The prep brief opens automatically. Review it before the session and flag any changes.
G

Ingest a Transcript

▶
What you say to Claude
"Here's the transcript from today's session with publishers [paste or reference Plaud recording]"
What Claude does
  • Extracts key decisions, feedback items, and action items from the transcript
  • Creates a Meeting Log entry with structured notes
  • Routes product feedback to the Feedback Tracker
  • Updates Partner Roster with any new information (contacts, preferences, concerns)
  • Creates Jira tickets for actionable product or engineering items
  • Flags items that need follow-up from specific team members
Where to verify
Check Meeting Log, Feedback Tracker, and Jira for the new entries. Claude will summarize what was routed where.
H

Check Program Health

▶
What you say to Claude
"How's the Council program looking?"
What Claude does
  • Reads the Partner Roster — how many confirmed vs. pending vs. declined
  • Checks the Feedback Tracker — volume, unprocessed items, trends
  • Reviews Collateral Status — what materials are ready vs. in progress
  • Checks the timeline — upcoming deadlines and milestones
  • Produces a concise health summary with any red flags
Where to verify
Claude will present the health summary directly. For deeper detail, check the Ops Dashboard.

6 Key Rules

These rules apply to all Council communications and operations. Non-negotiable.

  • All external communications must be approved by Steve before sending. Claude will draft and present — Steve sends or approves.
  • Book Intelligence describes what is publicly known about books. Never say "what's in the book" — Bookmarked does not read book contents.
  • Content Scanner reads actual book content with publisher permission. This is a separate, future capability — do not conflate with Book Intelligence.
  • Never position Bookmarked as replacing librarians. Bookmarked empowers librarians with better tools and data.
  • Lead with empowerment, not compliance burden. Time savings framing, not regulatory pressure.
  • Use "co-design" language. This is a Council, not a "focus group" or "beta test." Partners are collaborators, not subjects.
  • District data boundaries are sacred. Never cross district data boundaries or suggest doing so.
