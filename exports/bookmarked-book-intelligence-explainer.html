Understanding Bookmarked's Book Intelligence

How we help districts make more informed decisions about the books they order — and why that's fundamentally different from typing a title into ChatGPT.

What We Do

Research at Scale — So Your Team Doesn't Have To

Bookmarked uses AI to search the internet for publicly available information about books — professional reviews, news coverage, what educators and organizations have published. We collect that information and provide it to your district so your team can make more informed decisions about whether to order a book.

Instead of your staff searching the internet for each book one at a time, we do that research for them. For a list of hundreds or thousands of titles, that's the difference between weeks of manual effort and a single, consistent report.

How It's Different

Why Bookmarked Is Not the Same as ChatGPT

General AI tools like ChatGPT are impressive, but they weren't built for this job. Here's what makes Bookmarked's approach fundamentally different.

🔄

Consistency — One System, One Answer

When a librarian types a book title into ChatGPT, they get a result. When another librarian types the same title with a slightly different prompt, they get a different result. Even the same person using the same tool with the exact same prompt can get different answers — because large language models generate responses based on how they were trained, and that changes over time.

Multiply that across five staff members, three different AI tools, and thousands of titles, and you have a patchwork of contradicting information — none of it consistent, none of it comparable.

Bookmarked gives every reviewer the same intelligence, in the same framework, every time. One system. One answer. That's what your board and your team need to work from.

📖

Honesty About What AI Can and Can't Know

Here's something most people don't realize: no AI tool has the copyright ability to read what's actually inside a book — especially new titles. When you type a book title into ChatGPT, it's not reading the book. It's generating a response based on training data that may be incomplete, outdated, or entirely fabricated.

Bookmarked takes a different approach. We search for information about the book that's publicly available — what people have said, what's been published, what professionals have assessed. We never claim to know what's in a book. We tell you what we found, and if we didn't find much, we tell you that too.

That honesty matters. ChatGPT will confidently give you an answer about a brand-new book that may be completely made up. Your team deserves to know the difference between real information and a confident guess.

🔍

ISBN Fidelity — The Right Book, Not "A" Book

Many districts have policies requiring ISBN-based lookup — and for good reason. A book title alone can refer to multiple editions, formats, and versions, each with different content.

ChatGPT doesn't distinguish between ISBNs. It treats "a book called X" as one thing. Bookmarked ties intelligence to the specific ISBN — the exact edition your district is considering. That precision is what your process requires.

👥

Collaboration — One Platform, Not a Hundred Spreadsheets

In many districts today, book review data lives in spreadsheets, email threads, and individual notes. None of it is shared. None of it is searchable. When a committee needs to review a title, they're starting from scratch.

Bookmarked gives your librarians and staff a single platform to enter notes, share feedback, and collaborate with their committee and board. Every piece of information is captured in one place — accessible to everyone who needs it.

When a librarian transfers or retires, the institutional knowledge stays. That's a fundamentally different model from individual ChatGPT conversations that disappear when someone closes a browser tab.

Where We're Headed

The Publisher Partnership

We're building partnerships with publishers to create a secure environment where they provide us with the book itself. Our AI reads the book and generates intelligence that helps districts make informed ordering decisions according to their policies.

This model protects publishers and authors. We would never release copyright information publicly. Their job is to create, publish, and sell books. Our job is to help districts make informed decisions. The partnership serves both.

This is fundamentally different from what a general AI tool can offer. Publishers aren't going to hand their content to a general-purpose chatbot. But they will work with a platform built specifically to protect their intellectual property while helping districts make better decisions.
