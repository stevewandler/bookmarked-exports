Charlie,

You're right — I overcomplicated the last version. Before I show you the cleaned-up terms, I want to explain what I was trying to get at, because the intent was sound even if the execution missed.

The core problem I was trying to solve: ESC 16's current SLA already includes marketing, sales, and conference commitments, but none of it is happening. Their marketing activity is a gap. Their conference presence is a gap. Their lead generation is a gap. Derek took almost a week to send the Conroe ILA. These are real operational issues that cost us deals and slow down revenue.

What each element was trying to solve
  • Base vs. Accelerated rate (10%/15%) → Reward ESC 16 for actually helping close deals instead of just processing invoices. Right now they get paid the same whether they do nothing or actively sell.
  • Floor tied to marketing deliverables → Make the $75K guarantee contingent on ESC 16 actually participating in marketing, since the current SLA has zero accountability and they're not doing it.
  • Training tiers → Give ESC 16 a way to earn revenue beyond commission, making the partnership more valuable to them and giving them a reason to be more active.
  • Attribution automation → If we differentiate rates, we need a fair way to track who did what. Jan flagged gaming concerns and she's right.

Where I went wrong: I tried to solve all of these in the deal structure instead of solving them over time. You said it on Feb 17 — "Do I care whether they deliver on marketing? We're in the ballpark for just the invoicing and contracting piece." You were right. The 10% is fair for procurement. The marketing trial year gives us a full year of data. If ESC 16 delivers on marketing, we build incentives into Year 2. If they don't, we have leverage.

The problems I was trying to solve are real, but the right time to solve them is after we have data — not in a deal Nathan has already told us three times to keep simple.

Here are the terms stripped back to what we discussed. Ready to send to Nathan once you confirm.
