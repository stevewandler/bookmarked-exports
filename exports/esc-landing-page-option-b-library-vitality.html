The Problem

What Happened to Texas Libraries

Compliance paperwork has buried the profession. 26 states passed legislation creating operationally impossible requirements. In Texas, SB 13 didn't just create more work — it made book compliance a board-level liability.

The result? Books aren't being banned. They're just not being ordered. Librarians are so consumed by compliance that the core mission — matching the right book with the right reader — has been pushed aside.

That's not a library problem. That's a district-wide data infrastructure problem.

Compliance is consuming what it was meant to protect.
