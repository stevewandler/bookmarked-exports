The Feedback Loop — How This System Gets Better

This infrastructure was built in 3 days. It will not be perfect. That is by design. Your job is to use the system and tell Steve what feels wrong. Here is the loop:

  1. You notice something — a skill does not trigger right, output is confusing, a cadence feels off, data is missing
  2. You tell Steve — in your 1:1, Slack, or email. Just describe what happened and what you expected
  3. Steve brings it to Claude — he opens a thread and says "Sarah reported X, fix it"
  4. The system improves — next time you use it, the fix is live

You do not need to modify any skills, files, or configurations. You just use the system and report what you see. Your observations are the most valuable input.

2 Your Skills Toolkit

Six skills you will use regularly. All are accessed by typing the command in Claude Code.

/diagnostic

Do this first — one time

15-minute questionnaire about your workflows, tools, and frustrations. Calibrates all other skills to how you actually work. Only needs to be done once (unless you want to re-run after major process changes).

/jira

Every time you create a ticket

Describe what you need in plain language. Claude converts it into a properly structured Jira ticket with acceptance criteria, quality standards, and correct fields. Always use this instead of creating tickets directly in Jira.

/jira-standards

Before sprint entry

Validates that tickets are complete enough for engineering. This is the entry gate. Run it on any ticket before adding to a sprint. It will flag missing acceptance criteria, unclear scope, or incomplete requirements.

/feedback

After customer conversations

Structured input for customer insights. Walk through prompts about what you heard, from whom, and what it means. Routes insights to the Customer Feedback log in Confluence automatically.

/scrum-master

Sprint operations

Sprint planning prep, health monitoring, standup briefs, and retrospective data. Use it Monday for planning, daily for health checks, and at sprint close for the retrospective.

/cpo

Product strategy questions

Coaching frameworks for discovery, prioritization, and product thinking. When you are unsure how to approach a product decision, this skill walks you through structured frameworks.

How to Use a Skill

Open Claude Code on your computer. In the prompt, type the skill name (e.g., /jira) followed by what you need. Claude will guide you from there. You do not need to know any technical details — just describe what you want in plain language.

5 How the Pieces Fit Together

The system has a flow. Here is how work moves through it.

1

You hear something from a customer or identify a need

A customer call, a support ticket, a pattern you notice, a feature request. This is the raw input.

2

Log it with /feedback

Capture the insight while it is fresh. Claude structures it and routes it to the Customer Feedback log in Confluence. This builds your evidence base.

3

Turn it into work with /jira

When insight becomes action, describe what you need. Claude creates a properly structured ticket with acceptance criteria and quality standards baked in.

4

Gate it with /jira-standards

Before the ticket enters a sprint, validate it meets the quality bar. This prevents half-baked work from reaching engineering.

5

Plan the sprint with /scrum-master

Monday sprint planning. The skill preps capacity, priorities, and dependencies. You validate and commit.

6

Monitor and report in your 1:1

Thursday 1:1 with Steve. Review the scorecard (5 measurables), discuss rocks, IDS blockers, share customer insights, and align on cascading messages for the team.

7

Close and learn at sprint end

Sprint report auto-generates. Review carry-over, note patterns, feed lessons into the next cycle. Record key decisions in the Decisions Log.

6 What You Own vs. What Steve Owns

Clear boundaries so you know where to act independently and where to escalate.

Sarah Owns

  • Sprint planning and execution
  • Ticket quality (using /jira and /jira-standards)
  • Customer feedback capture and logging
  • Daily sprint health monitoring
  • Coordinating with Patrick on QA/UAT
  • Preparing for and driving the 1:1 agenda
  • Flagging blockers and risks early

Steve Owns

  • Product strategy and prioritization decisions
  • Modifying any skills, agents, or system files
  • Brand standards and messaging
  • Scorecard target-setting
  • Escalation resolution
  • System configuration and infrastructure changes
  • Hiring, partnerships, and external commitments

Important Boundary

If you ask Claude to change how a skill works, modify system files, or adjust agent behavior, Claude will say: "That's a change that needs Steve's approval. I'll flag it for him." This is intentional. You can use everything freely — you just cannot change the system itself. Tell Steve what you want changed and he will handle it.

8 Common Questions

Things you will probably wonder in your first two weeks.

What if a skill gives me bad output?

Tell Steve. Describe what you typed, what you got, and what you expected. He will fix it — usually within the same day.

Can I create tickets directly in Jira?

You can, but you should not. The /jira skill enforces quality standards that catch gaps before engineering sees them. Use the skill.

What if I do not have Claude Code set up?

Tell Steve immediately. You cannot use any skills without it. Setup takes about 15 minutes with the onboarding guide.

Do I need to update Confluence pages manually?

Most pages auto-populate through skills. The exceptions: your Operating Page (review for accuracy) and the Decisions Log (you add context after decisions are made).

What is the difference between this guide and the playbook?

The playbook (already shared) explains the "why" — product thinking, frameworks, your role in the cascade. This guide is the "how" — what to click, when to click it, in what order.

What if I disagree with how something works?

Good. That means you are paying attention. Write it down, bring it to your 1:1. The system is built to evolve based on your input.
