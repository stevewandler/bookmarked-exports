LIBRARY VITALITY

Libraries Should Be the Most Vibrant Rooms in a School

Steve Wandler · March 16, 2026 · 5 min read

Walk into most school libraries today and you won't find what you expect. Instead of buzzing reading groups and curious kids browsing shelves, you'll find a librarian buried in spreadsheets — tracking compliance paperwork that used to take minutes and now takes weeks.

When SB 13 passed in Texas, it didn't just create more work for librarians. It made book compliance a board-level liability. Suddenly, every challenged title required documentation, review committees, board reports, and defensible records. The tools hadn't changed, but the stakes had.

The result? Libraries became compliance offices. The most vibrant rooms in a school went quiet — not because students stopped reading, but because the adults who champion reading were drowning in process.

Your librarians should be matching books with readers — not managing spreadsheets.

That's why we built Bookmarked. Not as another library management system — the world has enough of those. We built a book intelligence platform that understands books the way librarians do: by content, by context, by community fit. Legacy systems list books. Bookmarked understands them.

The right book in the right hands can change a life. We believe that. And we believe the people best equipped to make that match are librarians — when they have the time and tools to do it well.
